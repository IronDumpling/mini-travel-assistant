Here’s a well-balanced 5-day ski trip itinerary for four people in the Swiss Alps, combining world-class skiing, stunning scenery, and après-ski fun.  

### **Day 1: Arrival in Zermatt**  
- **Morning:** Fly into Geneva or Zurich, then take the scenic train to **Zermatt** (4-5 hours).  
- **Afternoon:** Check into a cozy chalet or a hotel like **Hotel Schweizerhof** or **The Omnia**.  
- **Evening:** Explore car-free Zermatt, enjoy Swiss fondue at **Chez Vrony**, and rest up for skiing.  

### **Day 2: Skiing the Matterhorn Glacier Paradise**  
- **Morning:** Grab ski passes and hit **Matterhorn Glacier Paradise** (Europe’s highest ski area). Warm up on blues like **Gandegg**.  
- **Lunch:** Dine with Matterhorn views at **Restaurant Chez Vrony** (Roti).  
- **Afternoon:** Explore red runs like **Trockener Steg** or take the Gornergrat railway for more slopes.  
- **Evening:** Après-ski at **Elsie’s Bar**, then relax at a spa.  

### **Day 3: Transfer to Verbier (4 Vallées)**  
- **Morning:** Early train/bus to **Verbier** (2.5 hours), check into **W Hotel Verbier** or a luxury chalet.  
- **Afternoon:** Ski the famous **4 Vallées**, tackling runs like **Gentianes** or **Lac des Vaux**.  
- **Evening:** Drinks at **Le Rouge**, then dinner at **La Table d’Adrien**.  

### **Day 4: Off-Piste & Mont Fort Adventure**  
- **Morning:** Hire a guide for off-piste skiing near **Mont Fort** or try the **Tortin** mogul field.  
- **Lunch:** Chalet-style meal at **Cabane Mont Fort**.  
- **Afternoon:** More skiing or snowshoeing for a break.  
- **Evening:** Celebrate with cocktails at **Farinet Lounge** and live music.  

### **Day 5: Final Runs & Departure**  
- **Morning:** Last runs in Verbier or a scenic helicopter tour over the Alps.  
- **Afternoon:** Train back to Geneva/Zurich (2.5–3 hours), fly home.  

### **Budget Estimate (Per Person):**  
- **Flights:** $600–$1,200 (from US/EU)  
- **Trains/Transfers:** $200–$300  
- **Ski Passes:** $300–$400 (5-day regional pass)  
- **Accommodation:** $1,000–$2,500 (mid-range to luxury)  
- **Food/Drinks:** $500–$800  
- **Extras (guides, spa, etc.):** $200–$500  

**Total:** ~$3,000–$6,000 per person (depending on luxury level).  

### **Tips:**  
- Book ski passes/accommodation early (February is peak season).  
- Pack layers—Swiss Alps weather can change quickly.  
- Consider travel insurance for ski coverage.
