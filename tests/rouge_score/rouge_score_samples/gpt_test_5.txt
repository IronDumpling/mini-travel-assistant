### Transport Tips (Be Suspicious of Big Passes)

* **Prefer buses** like FlixBus, Megabus, or student‑friendly ride‑shares (e.g., BlaBlaCar) over expensive rail passes ([The Roaming Renegades][2]).
* **Budget airlines** such as Ryanair, EasyJet, WizzAir can be surprisingly cheap if booked early and travel light ([The Roaming Renegades][2]).
* **Interrail/youth passes** can be convenient, but they cost £100–200/day overall—even with discounts—so they may bust your budget ¬ unless heavily used ([泰晤士报][3]).

### Sample 14‑Day Itinerary (Lean Eastern, Touch Western)

**Days 1–4: Prague, Czech Republic**

* Dorm-hostel: \~\$20–30/night
* Eat from grocery stores or street markets
* Free walking tours (tip-based)

**Days 5–7: Budapest, Hungary**

* Similar pricing to Prague
* Relaxed sightseeing + thermal baths

**Days 8–9: Kraków, Poland**

* Even lower cost (\~\$15–25/day)
* Explore the Old Town and nearby nature

**Days 10–11: Vienna, Austria**

* A bit pricier: expect \~\$35–45/day
* Use cheap bus/ride-share routes

**Days 12–14: Bratislava, Slovakia or Ljubljana, Slovenia**

* Comparable to Western Europe but manageable via cheap transport
* End with two relaxed days before heading back

Alternatively, if you want one Western European city (e.g., Berlin or Barcelona), allocate 2–3 days at \~\$100/day and compensate with more days in cheaper Eastern cities.

### Money‑Saving Strategies

* **Book hostels early**, focus on ones with free breakfast (cuts meal costs) ([YouTube][4], [泰晤士报][3], [Voyista][5], [Reddit][6]).
* **Cook your own food** or rely on groceries—cooking saves \$100–200 per week ([Under The Sun][7]).
* **Use free walking tours**, tipping only as your means allow ([泰晤士报][3]).
* **Stay near smaller towns**, not always city center—stays in places like Neustrelitz or Roggwil can be charming and cost-effective ([businessinsider.com][8]).
